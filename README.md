    <h2>Etymology</h2>
    <p>
       The word comes from Greek epistēmē, meaning “knowledge,” and logos, meaning “study” or “reason.” It is connected to epistemology, which is a branch of philosophy that studies knowledge.

    </p>

    <h2>Definition</h2>
    <p>
        Epistemological refers to ideas or questions related to the nature, source, and limits of knowledge — in other words, how we know what we know.

    </p>

    <h3>Denotation</h3>
    <p>
        The denotation is the literal, objective meaning:
    <ul>
        <li>Relating to the study of knowledge and understanding.
</li>
        <li</li>
    </ul>

    <div class="example">
        <strong>Example (Denotation):</strong><br>
       The professor discussed epistemological theories about how humans recognize truth.
    </div>

    <h3>Connotation</h3>
    <p>
      The word sounds formal, academic, and intellectual. It often gives a serious or scholarly tone.
    </p>
    <ul>
        <li</li>
        <li</li>
    </ul>

        <strong>Example (Connotation):</strong><br>
